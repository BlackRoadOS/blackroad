## The Economic Model

### Revenue Sources (Not Token Sales)
| Source | Revenue | Who Pays |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Subscriptions ($10-100/mo) | User fees | Consumers |
| Enterprise ($300-8,000/mo) | Corporate fees | Businesses stealing from incumbents |
| x402 micropayments (0.1% fee) | Agent economy | AI agents paying each other |
| Coinbase Commerce (RoadCoin purchases) | Token sales | Users wanting more ROAD |
| Staking rewards (funded by treasury) | Treasury yield | Long-term sustainability |

### Why It's Not a Ponzi
- RoadCoin has **utility** (spend on real features: inference, agents, memory)
- Value comes from **platform usage** (more users = more demand for ROAD)
- Supply is **fixed** (1B, no infinite printing)
- Revenue comes from **subscriptions + enterprise**, not just token sales
- The token SUPPLEMENTS the business model, it doesn't replace it
- If RoadCoin goes to zero, the platform still works on fiat subscriptions
